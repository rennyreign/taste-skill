# Sorted Local Site Refresh

## Purpose
Build clean, modern, local-business websites from mockups, screenshots, existing websites, or brief notes.

This skill adapts Taste Skill's premium frontend taste to Sorted's commercial reality: fast, practical, local, trustworthy, and easy for a small business owner to say yes to.

The goal is not maximum creative exploration. The goal is a sharp, credible site that feels like the best version of the business.

## Operating principle
Use the mockup as the first source of truth, then improve it only where the upgrade makes the business clearer, more credible, or more desirable.

Think in two deliverable tiers:

1. Standard: faithful, clean, polished, fast.
2. Premium: same business and structure, but richer visual presence, stronger motion, refined typography, better image treatment, and more memorable section rhythm.

Premium must not become a different brand. It should feel like the same business after a proper glow-up, not like a local garage wearing a Silicon Valley costume.

## Workflow

### 1. Identify the business type
Classify the project before designing or coding:

- local service business
- trade / repair specialist
- fitness / martial arts / wellness
- beauty / personal care
- food / hospitality
- professional service
- ecommerce-led local business
- ecommerce/service hybrid
- booking-led business
- trust-led business

Then define the primary conversion action:

- call now
- book intro / consultation
- request quote
- visit shop
- buy product
- join class
- send WhatsApp / message
- view timetable / services

### 2. Analyse the mockup or existing site
Before implementation, extract:

- section order
- navigation labels
- hero message
- CTA labels
- brand colours
- typography mood
- button style
- image treatment
- icon style
- trust signals
- products or services
- social proof
- opening times, location, phone, email
- footer/legal needs
- mobile risks

If details are unclear, preserve the visible design language and make the most practical local-business choice.

### 3. Choose Standard or Premium
If the user does not specify, produce the Standard version first and note clear Premium upgrade opportunities.

Standard should:

- stay close to the provided mockup
- improve spacing, hierarchy, responsive layout, and CTA clarity
- use clean hover states and simple transitions
- avoid heavy animation or complex effects
- be buildable quickly
- feel credible and complete

Premium should:

- retain the same business, content, and conversion goal
- improve hero composition and visual impact
- add refined typography and richer section rhythm
- add tasteful animation, transitions, reveal timing, and hover states
- improve image grading, crops, overlays, and depth
- add subtle texture, ambient backgrounds, or motion only when useful
- make the business feel more desirable without making it feel fake

### 4. Select a local-business page pattern
Use a proven pattern instead of inventing from scratch.

Common service-site pattern:
1. header/navigation
2. hero with primary CTA and clear value
3. trust bar or quick proof
4. services/programmes/products
5. why choose us / benefits
6. featured audience or service section
7. about/location credibility block
8. testimonials/reviews
9. contact/location/booking CTA
10. footer

Common ecommerce/service hybrid pattern:
1. header/navigation
2. hero with call/shop CTA
3. trust bar: warranty, quality, delivery, support
4. shop/service entry cards
5. social proof or latest work
6. product grid or featured products
7. why choose us
8. brands/categories supported
9. contact/help CTA
10. footer

Common booking-led pattern:
1. header/navigation
2. hero with booking CTA
3. service cards
4. how it works
5. proof/results
6. pricing or packages if appropriate
7. FAQ
8. contact/location CTA
9. footer

### 5. Apply Sorted design standards
Every site should feel:

- obvious
- useful
- trustworthy
- frictionless
- local
- human
- competent
- polished

Avoid:

- startup language
- AI language
- agency language
- consultant language
- abstract SaaS visuals
- fake dashboards
- overdesigned micro-labels
- excessive purple/blue AI gradients
- unreadable text overlays
- generic three-card rows when a more useful layout fits
- visual cleverness that hides core business information

### 6. Copy rules
Write plain UK English unless the business clearly needs another tone.

Prefer:

- Book a free intro
- Call us now
- View timetable
- Find the right part
- Request a quote
- Fast UK-wide delivery
- Classes for beginners, kids and competitors
- Clear prices. Quick turnaround. No fuss.

Avoid:

- elevate
- unleash
- transform your digital presence
- next-gen
- seamless
- revolutionary
- cutting-edge solutions
- empower your journey
- world-class experience unless specifically credible

Do not use lorem ipsum. Use plausible draft copy tailored to the business.

### 7. Motion and interaction rules
Maintain aesthetics, animation, and clean transitions, but match the business.

Standard motion:

- button hover and active states
- card hover lift or border shift
- smooth nav/anchor scrolling
- simple staggered section reveals
- subtle image zoom on hover where useful
- form focus states

Premium motion:

- refined hero entrance sequence
- staggered cards and section reveals
- scroll-triggered image/text reveals
- subtle parallax or background drift
- active nav states
- richer hover transitions
- motion using transform and opacity only

Never animate layout properties such as top, left, width, or height. Avoid motion that makes the business feel gimmicky.

### 8. Implementation standards
Build with the existing stack where one exists. Do not migrate frameworks for a refresh.

Always include:

- responsive desktop/tablet/mobile layout
- semantic HTML landmarks
- accessible buttons and links
- visible focus states
- useful alt text
- real draft copy
- correct contact/location placement when known
- footer with privacy and terms links
- active navigation state where applicable
- metadata: title and description
- no dead placeholder links unless clearly marked
- no commented-out dead code

If using third-party packages, check dependencies first. Do not assume animation, icon, or UI libraries exist.

## Quality checklist
Before final output, verify:

- Is the primary CTA obvious within 5 seconds?
- Does the hero explain what the business does and who it is for?
- Are phone, booking, location, or purchase paths easy to find?
- Does the design match the mockup enough to feel familiar?
- Does the premium version feel upgraded without changing the business category?
- Is the page mobile-safe and free of horizontal scroll?
- Are all sections useful rather than decorative filler?
- Is the copy plain, local, and believable?
- Are animations smooth, restrained, and performance-safe?
- Does the result feel like something a real small business owner would proudly approve?

## Final response behaviour
When presenting work, state whether the output is Standard or Premium.

If both are requested, describe the difference in practical terms:

- Standard: close to the mockup, clean, fast, reliable.
- Premium: same foundation with stronger visual treatment, richer motion, and more polished sections.

Do not oversell. Be clear, calm, and useful.
